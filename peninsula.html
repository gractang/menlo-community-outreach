Life Moves

The homeless population that we are privileged to serve is facing unique and serious risks associated with COVID-19. LifeMoves is working 24/7 to support our clients, our staff, and our community. We’ve had significant growth in our costs to support staff, order supplies and arrange for food. In response, and to support the sharp increase in costs associated with protecting our clients and the unsheltered during this pandemic, we have created the COVID-19 Emergency Relief Fund with a matching gift from a group of anonymous donors. The Fund will support unexpected costs ranging from providing meals, buying personal protective equipment, covering professional deep cleaning services and supporting our compassionate shelter staff with overtime pay. Your generosity really helps in the face of these unanticipated expenses. In addition, We are quickly running out of supplies. It would be extremely valuable to our shelters and staff on the front line if each household could donate just one bottle of sanitizer plus one roll of paper towel/toilet roll.

Organizational contact: Nandini Gondhalekar, Director of Individual Giving (ngondhalekar@lifemoves.org)


Peninsula Bridge

The most vulnerable populations are truly getting hit the hardest. Our Peninsula Bridge students and families are really suffering. They are facing lay-offs, working in unsafe conditions, and inadequate access to medical care. Many students don't have the technology and Wi-Fi to connect to the internet from home. While this is not directly part of our core mission, it is an emergency situation and our families are facing acute need right now. Hoover Community School

Hoover Community School currently serves 740 Transitional-Kindergarten through 8th Grade students in the Redwood City School District. Seventy percent are English Learners, 94% are Hispanic and 11% are Special Education. As a Community School, the school, families, and community partners work together to support the whole child and their families. Our low-income families face extreme economic challenges in this area. With approximately 95% of our students receiving free and reduced lunch and rents skyrocketing in the area, our families are struggling to stay in the area. We continue to tap into all of our community resources to support our families in greatest need. Some students have attendance challenges and some are homeless. Every day our teachers are committed to providing the best education for our students and laying the foundation for college and career. Despite the daily challenges our families face, our students have come to school ready to learn and prepare for their future. We now face an additional challenge, that of the great digital divide during shelter-in-place. They need iPad and internet access for remote learning. We greatly appreciate any support in our efforts to provide our students with the tools they need to learn under these trying times. Please feel to contact me at (408) 930-5953. Thank you for your consideration.

Organizational contact: Lupe Guzman, Community School Coordinator (lguzman@rcsdk8.net)
